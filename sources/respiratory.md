# Respiratory — core facts

## Asthma

Asthma is a chronic inflammatory airway disease characterised by reversible airflow obstruction. The diagnosis is supported by demonstrating an improvement in FEV1 of at least 12 percent after a bronchodilator.

The first step in the management of an acute asthma attack is high-flow oxygen together with a nebulised short-acting beta-2 agonist such as salbutamol.

## Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease

Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease is most commonly caused by smoking. Spirometry showing a post-bronchodilator FEV1 to FVC ratio below 0.7 confirms airflow obstruction.

## Pneumonia

Community-acquired pneumonia is most commonly caused by Streptococcus pneumoniae. The CURB-65 score is used to assess severity and guide whether a patient can be managed at home or needs admission.
