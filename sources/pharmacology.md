# Pharmacology — core facts

## Beta-blockers

Beta-blockers reduce heart rate and myocardial oxygen demand by blocking beta-1 adrenergic receptors. They are contraindicated in patients with severe asthma because they can precipitate bronchospasm.

Propranolol is a non-selective beta-blocker, whereas bisoprolol and metoprolol are cardioselective.

## Antibiotics

Amoxicillin is a beta-lactam antibiotic that inhibits bacterial cell wall synthesis. It should be avoided in patients with a documented penicillin allergy.

Gentamicin is an aminoglycoside that can cause nephrotoxicity and ototoxicity, so drug levels must be monitored during treatment.

## Anticoagulation

Warfarin inhibits vitamin K epoxide reductase and its effect is monitored using the international normalised ratio. The target INR for most indications, including atrial fibrillation, is between two and three.

Direct oral anticoagulants such as apixaban do not require routine INR monitoring.
